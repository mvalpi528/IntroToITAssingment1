Project Idea

Overview

The project that I would like to undertake is the creation of an iOS application that assists music students with applying the principles of ‘deliberate practice’ to their music practice at home. ‘Deliberate practice’ as described by Anders Ericsson and Robert Pool in their book Peak (reference) is a way of practicing that is focused and effortful, incorporating external feedback to inform appropriate adjustments to a student’s technique and output.

As deliberate practice requires feedback, it is necessary for the application to be ‘smart’. In this context I am using the word ‘smart’ to refer to the ability to accept and analyse user inputs in order to generate and display useful feedback to the student.

In much the same way as a music teacher is able to give feedback, the application should be able to listen to a student’s performance and give appropriate and specific feedback across a number of areas, such as pitch, rhythm and tuning.

Motivation

Many music students struggle to practice effectively without the direct guidance of a teacher. As the majority of students' time practicing their musical instrument is without the guidance of a teacher, there is a large opportunity to improve in the quality of this practice time, leading to significant improvements in educational outcomes for students.

Other broader trends that suggest a use case for this technology is the fact that instrumental music programs continue to be popular in Australian schools and that children routinely use iPads for classroom learning.

There are many adults that are turning toward technology to learn music and as a result a number of technological solutions have emerged to support independent music learning, especially of instruments such as guitar and piano. However, many of these apps only show a student what to play, not necessarily how to practice for maximum results.

Description

Parental lock

A major concern with leaving young students alone with iPads is that they will be tempted to use the iPads to play games or watch videos. A solution for this would be to create a parental lock feature that would not allow the student to access other apps for a given amount of time or until a parent enters a password, stopping students from exiting the app and playing games during their practice time.

Metronome

The metronome is a vital practice tool that plays a ‘click’ sound at regular intervals to help musicians practice keeping a steady pulse. This tool already exists in a variety of digital forms and could be feasibly integrated as a feature in a broader application.

Tuner

Integrating a tuner will help students tune their instruments without the help of a teacher. Tuners can also be used to practice creating and sustaining a given pitch on instruments that don't have frets or keys. Many sophisticated digital tuners already exist in the marketplace with some including detailed analysis modes that can give you an accuracy percentage.

Display sheet music + MIDI playback

Many music notation and creation programs use the Musical Instrument Digital Interface (MIDI) format. This format uses digital inputs to trigger audio samples or software instruments, giving the impression of a musical performance. Integration of MIDI functionality into the application will allow the app to demonstrate how a particular piece or section of music should sound.

Another option that may be slightly less realistic is the idea of syncing audio to pdf sheet music and superimposing a playback marker that shows where the audio syncs to the sheet music. However, I’m not sure how technically feasible this is as it is important that the audio can be sped up and slowed down to match the students current maximum performance speed.

Audio analysis

I expect that this will be the most difficult aspect of the project to implement. Although software that can analyse audio already exists, I can imagine it would be incredibly difficult to create a program that can make sense of audio and give appropriate recommendations to correct student mistakes.

Practice timer/log

This feature will record and log the amount of time spent in the app, keeping track of the amount of time the student has spent practicing. This is useful for teachers, students and parents alike who would all have a vested interest in making sure that the student practices regularly. This can create a sense of pride and accountability for students who will see their total hours increase over time.

Badges and social proof

This would bring an element of gamification to the app. Gamification is a frequently used strategy to increase user engagement and often involves fostering a sense of achievement by guiding a user to complete discrete challenges or pass certain milestones. Gamification is often implemented by creating levels that a user can advance through or by presenting the user with badges or awards. In this case, badges are likely to be the more relevant way of gamifying this particular app. An example of this may be awarding the user a badge for ‘100 hours of practice’ or ‘7 days in a row’ of practice. These could be displayed on the students profile as a means of social proof and a way for students to showcase their accomplishments.

Tools and Technologies

As iOS apps are created in XCode, a macOS application, a Macintosh computer will be required to write the code. Although XCode is able to test your code using virtual devices, it would also be prudent to test the app on a variety of iPads and iPhones to the real end user experience. Another reason for using real devices as opposed to a simulation is to test the functionality that requires the devices microphone and speakers.

The application will also require some kind of remote storage for user data. The data that will need to be stored includes the users sheet music and audio files as well as user metadata such as the number of times they have logged in and how much time they have spent in the app.

Skills Required

    Programming in Swift and or Objective C. These are the primary languages for iOS development and will be required to create an iOS app.

    Reading sheet music and other basic music literacy concepts. This is vital to assess whether the application actually works and achieves its desired result.

    How to navigate and use iOS devices. This seems like it would be assumed knowledge, but it is necessary to be able to test the product.

    Creating effective databases and managing cloud storage. This is necessary to ensure that user data is stored in a secure and cost effective way.

Outcome

The desired outcome of this project is that students will not only practice more effectively, but also that they will become more likely to practice through the use of gamification and other psychological principles that encourage repeated and sustained usage.

Through more frequent and higher quality practice students will progress faster at their given instrument. Success in any field makes participants more likely to continue as a result of their feelings of mastery and satisfaction at completing difficult tasks with skill. Hopeful these factors will all combine to create more confident and capable musicians.
